What is Car Teflon Coating?

Teflon coating — scientifically known as PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene) — is a synthetic, non-stick protective film applied to your car's exterior paint. The same remarkable material used in non-stick cookware, PTFE creates a slick, water-repellent barrier over your vehicle's clear coat.

In practical terms: Teflon coating wraps your paint in an invisible 0.2-micron shield that repels water and dirt, resists minor scratches and swirl marks, protects against UV fading, and delivers a rich showroom-quality gloss — for up to 6 to 8 months from a single professional application.

At DRK Stickers in Coimbatore, we apply premium-grade PTFE products using a meticulous 6-step process — ensuring even coverage, proper curing, and maximum longevity so your car's protection and shine actually last.

Aftercare Guide

How to Maximise Your Teflon Coating

Follow these simple aftercare practices to get the full 6–8 months from your Teflon coating and keep your car looking its best.

Important: Wait at least 48 hours after application before washing your car — this allows the PTFE coating to fully cure and bond with the paint surface.

1

Use pH-Neutral Car Shampoo

Always wash with a mild, pH-balanced car shampoo. Harsh detergents and alkaline cleaners break down the PTFE layer significantly faster, reducing protection life.

2

Avoid Automatic Brush Car Washes

Stiff rotating brushes in automatic car washes are the fastest way to wear down your Teflon coating. Use hand wash services or wash at home with soft microfibre mitts.

3

Park in Shade When Possible

While Teflon provides UV protection, minimising direct sunlight exposure when parked extends coating life — and protects the paint beneath from heat-related degradation.

4

Remove Bird Droppings Quickly

Even with Teflon protection, bird droppings and tree sap contain acids that can etch into paint if left too long. Remove them promptly with a damp microfibre cloth.

5

Use Soft Microfibre for Wiping

When wiping the surface, always use clean, soft microfibre cloths. Rough towels or sponges introduce micro-scratches that dull both the coating and the paint beneath.

6

Re-Apply Every 6–8 Months

For continuous, uninterrupted paint protection, we recommend returning for a fresh Teflon application every 6 to 8 months. Many customers book a regular slot with us each season.
