Understanding the product

What Are ACP Boards (Aluminium Composite Panels)?

ACP boards — short for Aluminium Composite Panels — are modern building and cladding materials made from two thin aluminium sheets bonded to a solid polyethylene or fire-retardant mineral core. The result is a panel that is remarkably strong for its weight, completely flat, and available in virtually any colour or surface finish.

ACP boards have become the first choice for architects, builders, shop owners, and interior designers across Coimbatore because they combine the clean, premium look of metal with the practical advantages of lightweight installation.

Whether you need exterior wall cladding for a commercial building, a sleek fascia for your shop signage, or a clean interior accent wall — ACP boards deliver a professional finish at a fraction of the cost of stone, glass, or solid aluminium.

Construction

What Makes an ACP Board — Layer by Layer

Understanding the construction of an aluminium composite panel helps you see why it performs so well in both interior and exterior applications.

Top Layer

Aluminium Skin (0.3mm – 0.5mm)

High-grade aluminium sheet coated with PVDF or polyester paint. Gives the panel its colour, texture, UV resistance, and scratch protection. This is what you see and touch.

Core Layer

PE or Fire-Retardant Mineral Core

The middle layer determines rigidity, strength, and fire rating. PE core for interior and low-rise use. FR mineral core for high-rise buildings and fire-safety-critical applications. This is what gives ACP its superior strength-to-weight ratio.

Base Layer

Reverse Aluminium Skin

A second aluminium sheet on the reverse completes the sandwich structure — preventing warping, maintaining flatness over time, and giving the panel its balanced rigidity.

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions About ACP Boards

What is the standard thickness of ACP boards?
ACP boards are commonly available in 3mm and 4mm thickness. For exterior cladding and large facades, 4mm panels are recommended. 3mm panels are suitable for interior applications and signage work.
What is the difference between PE core and FR core ACP?
PE (Polyethylene) core panels are lighter and more affordable — suitable for interior use and low-rise exteriors. FR (Fire Retardant) core panels have a mineral-filled centre that slows fire spread — required for high-rise and public buildings.
Are ACP boards suitable for Coimbatore's climate?
Yes. Quality ACP boards with PVDF coating handle UV exposure, monsoon rain, humidity, and thermal cycling — all highly relevant to Coimbatore's climate. We recommend PVDF-coated panels for all exterior installations here.
How long do ACP boards last outdoors?
With quality PVDF-coated ACP panels and proper installation, you can expect 15 to 20 years of service life with minimal colour fade or structural degradation.
Can ACP boards be used for shop signage?
Absolutely. ACP boards are the most popular substrate for flat signage, shop name boards, and backlit sign boxes. They are flat, lightweight, easy to cut, and accept vinyl graphics, paint, and printing with ease.
Can ACP panels be bent or shaped for custom features?
Yes. ACP boards can be routered and folded to create three-dimensional shapes, curved facades, column cladding, and custom architectural features. Our fabrication team handles all custom shaping in-house.
What sizes do ACP boards come in?
Standard ACP sheets are 1220mm × 2440mm (4 feet × 8 feet). We also supply larger sizes up to 1500mm × 5800mm for large facade projects. Custom cuts are available — we fabricate to your exact dimensions.
