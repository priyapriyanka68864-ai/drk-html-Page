Understanding the Service
What is Architectural Sun Control Film
— and How Does it Work?

Architectural sun control film is a specially engineered multi-layer window film applied directly to the inside surface of existing glass windows and doors — in homes, offices, shops, showrooms, apartment buildings and any space with glass that lets in unwanted heat, glare or UV radiation.

Unlike curtains or blinds that block your view entirely, architectural sun control films allow natural light and outward visibility while selectively blocking heat-producing infrared rays and harmful UV radiation that come with sunlight.

💡 Simple explanation: Sunlight carries three things — visible light (you need this), UV rays (harmful) and infrared heat (makes rooms hot). Sun control film blocks the UV and infrared while letting the visible light through. Cooler rooms. Bright interiors. Lower electricity bills. Zero structural changes to your building.

What Sun Control Film Blocks
☀️
Infrared Heat (IR)
The primary cause of hot rooms and high AC bills. High-performance films reject up to 80% of solar IR energy before it enters your space.
🌿
UV Radiation — Up to 99%
The invisible rays that fade your furniture, flooring and interiors. Premium films block up to 99% of harmful UVA and UVB radiation.
💡
Glare — Controlled
Reduces harsh glare on screens, eyes and interiors without blocking natural light entirely — keeping rooms bright but comfortable.
🔒
Outside Visibility (Privacy)
Reflective and darker tint films provide daytime privacy — outside cannot see in, inside can see out clearly.
