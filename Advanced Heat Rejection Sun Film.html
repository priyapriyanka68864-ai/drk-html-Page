What is Heat Rejection
Sun Film for Cars?

Heat rejection sun film is a specially engineered multi-layer film applied to your car's glass surfaces to control the amount of solar energy entering the cabin.

Unlike ordinary window tints that simply darken your glass, advanced heat rejection sun films use nano-ceramic or multi-layer optical technology to actively reject infrared heat, block harmful UV radiation and reduce glare — while maintaining clear, natural visibility.

When sunlight hits your car's glass, it carries three types of energy — visible light, UV rays and infrared heat. A premium heat rejection film from DRK Stickers intercepts a significant portion of this energy before it enters your cabin.

Visible Light (VLT)
Controlled by tint darkness level — affects privacy and aesthetics. Legal limits apply in Tamil Nadu.
UV Rays (Blocked up to 99%)
Harmful ultraviolet radiation that damages skin and causes interior fading. Premium films block up to 99%.
Infrared Heat (IR Rejection)
The main cause of cabin heating. Ceramic films reject the highest levels of IR energy — keeping your car dramatically cooler.
