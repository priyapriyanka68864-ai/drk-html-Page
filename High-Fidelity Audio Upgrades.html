The problem
Why factory audio falls short

Distortion at high volumes

Factory speakers are built to cost targets. They struggle with bass and treble simultaneously, resulting in muddy, distorted sound when you turn it up.

Weak bass response

Without a dedicated subwoofer, bass lines sound thin and flat. A custom car sound system with a proper sub transforms how music feels — not just sounds.

Poor soundstaging

Without correct speaker placement and DSP tuning, music sounds like it's coming from the dashboard rather than surrounding you in a natural soundstage.

No smart connectivity

Older factory head units lack Android Auto and Apple CarPlay — making navigation and hands-free calling inconvenient and unsafe.

Components explained
What goes into your sound system
Head unit

The brain

Controls all audio sources — FM, Bluetooth, USB, Android Auto, Apple CarPlay. Outputs a clean signal to your amplifier for the best possible downstream sound quality.

Component speakers

Clarity & imaging

Separate tweeters and woofers placed precisely for accurate soundstaging — vocals at ear level, instruments exactly where they belong in the stereo image.

Subwoofer

The foundation

Reproduces frequencies below 80Hz that regular speakers cannot. Kick drums you feel, bass lines you hear clearly — music that sounds complete rather than thin.

Amplifier

Clean power

Supplies stable, high-current power to your speakers. Eliminates distortion and clipping at high volumes. Unlocks full speaker performance without overtaxing the head unit.

DSP

The tuning tool

Calibrates time alignment (all speakers reaching your ears together), equalisation (matching cabin acoustics), and crossover points (right frequencies to right speakers).

Sound deadening

The quiet foundation

Applied to doors, floor, and boot panels to eliminate rattles, road noise, and vibration — giving your audio system a quiet, clean environment to perform at its best.

What is the difference between DSP tuning and a regular equaliser?
+
A standard EQ boosts or cuts frequencies. A DSP (Digital Signal Processor) goes much further — controlling time alignment (so sound from all speakers reaches your ears simultaneously), precise crossover points, and cabin-specific room correction for a dramatically more accurate soundstage.
