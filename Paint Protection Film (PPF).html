Understanding PPF
What is Paint Protection Film
— and How Does it Work?

Paint Protection Film — also called PPF, clear bra or transparent car paint protection — is a thick, optically clear thermoplastic urethane (TPU) film applied directly to your car's painted surfaces. Once professionally installed, the film is virtually invisible — but incredibly tough.

Think of PPF as an invisible armour for your car's paint. It sits on top of your factory finish and absorbs the physical impacts, scratches and environmental damage that would otherwise permanently scar your original paint. When it eventually gets damaged, you replace the film — not your car's paint.

🛡️ The Simple Analogy

PPF is like a phone screen protector — but engineered for car paint. It takes all the scratches, chips and damage so your original paint underneath stays permanently perfect. And like a screen protector, you replace the film when needed — never the original surface.

The 3 Layers of PPF — Explained
1
Top Layer
Self-Healing Clearcoat
Elastomeric polymer topcoat — heals minor scratches and swirl marks with heat from sunlight or warm water. Stain-resistant and hydrophobic.
2
Core Layer
TPU Armour — Thermoplastic Urethane
The armour layer — absorbs physical impact from stone chips, road debris and minor scratches. Prevents anything from reaching your factory paint.
3
Bottom Layer
Pressure-Sensitive Adhesive
Bonds the film securely to your paint — tight enough to last 10 years, clean enough to remove without any paint damage or residue.

Our Method
PPF Installation Process
— 6 Professional Steps

Professional PPF installation is a precision craft — requiring clean conditions, precision film cutting and expert wrapping technique. Every DRK Stickers installation follows this strict process:

01
Paint Inspection & Preparation Assessment

Full paint inspection under controlled lighting — checking for swirl marks, scratches, chips and contaminants. All defects are documented and discussed. Required paint correction is completed before PPF installation — because once the film goes on, it locks in whatever condition the paint is in.

02
Full Paint Decontamination & Cleaning

Complete foam pre-wash, hand wash and clay bar decontamination removes all surface dirt, dust, road tar and bonded contaminants. The paint must be perfectly clean before film application — zero trapped particles under the film is the standard we work to.

03
Precision Computer-Cut Film Preparation

Your PPF panels are precision-cut using a computer-aided cutting system mapped to the exact dimensions and contours of your specific car model. Every panel fits perfectly with clean edges and zero gaps — we never use imprecise hand-cut pieces that leave exposed edges.

04
Professional Film Application — Panel by Panel

Each panel is applied using professional slip solution and squeegee technique — carefully working the film onto the paint surface, stretching it around curves, edges and complex contour lines. All air bubbles and water pockets are removed for a completely smooth, invisible bond on every panel.

05
Edge Tucking & Heat Forming

All film edges are carefully tucked into door gaps, grille openings and panel edges — or heat-formed around curves using a heat gun — to ensure clean, invisible edges that will not lift or peel over time. This step is what separates a professional DRK Stickers installation from an amateur job.

06
Final Inspection, Curing & Delivery

Car inspected under controlled lighting for any lifting edges, bubbles or imperfections. Film allowed to cure 24–48 hours in studio. Some haze and small water pockets may be visible during curing — these clear completely as the film bonds fully. Written warranty certificate issued on delivery.
